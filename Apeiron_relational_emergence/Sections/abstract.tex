\begin{abstract}
The APEIRON Framework proposes a multi‑layer architecture for autonomous knowledge genesis that derives structure directly from elementary observables, without human‑provided labels or categories.
Previous work demonstrated that the relational hypergraph of Layer~2 and the Louvain‑based functional clustering of Layer~3 can recover meaningful pixel groupings from raw images (MNIST, CIFAR‑10).
Here we extend the same unsupervised pipeline to three non‑visual domains: electrocardiograms (ECG), financial time series, and seismic records.
In each case, we construct a co‑activation graph from sliding‑window representations, apply Louvain community detection, and assess the functional significance of the resulting clusters via counterfactual ablation.
The pipeline consistently discovers modules that align with known physiological, economic, or geophysical structures, confirming that the APEIRON substrate operates in a genuinely domain‑agnostic manner.
All experiments are fully reproducible via an accompanying Python script.
\end{abstract}
