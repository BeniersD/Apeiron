\section{Conclusion}

We have presented a multi-prover framework for formally verifying the atomicity of foundational observables in the \Apeiron{} Framework. By integrating SymPy, Z3, Lean~4, and Coq, the system can generate, cross-verify, and export machine-checkable proofs of atomicity across multiple mathematical frameworks. Experiments on canonical observables demonstrate the feasibility of the approach and the convergence of all provers.

This work represents a step toward rigorously grounded AI architectures, where the most basic units of representation are not merely heuristically defined but \textit{certified} by formal mathematics. While significant scalability challenges remain, the multi-prover architecture provides a flexible foundation for incremental, compositional verification as the field of formal methods continues to advance. The open-source release of \texttt{self\_proving.py} and its accompanying certificates invites the community to critique, extend, and build upon this foundation in the pursuit of truly trustworthy artificial intelligence.
