\begin{abstract}
The APEIRON Framework is a seventeen‑layer architecture for non‑anthropocentric knowledge genesis, whose foundational layer (Layer~1) defines irreducible observables via \emph{multi‑axial atomicity}: an observable is atomic if it simultaneously satisfies the atomicity criteria of four independent mathematical frameworks (Boolean algebra, measure theory, category theory, and information theory).
This paper gives a complete categorical formalisation of that core concept.
We define the \emph{Apeiron category} $\mathcal{A}$ in which objects are Ultimate Observables and morphisms are generated by the decomposition operators corresponding to the four frameworks.
A central result (Theorem~\ref{th:separated}) shows that multi‑axial atomicity of an observable is precisely equivalent to its being a \emph{separated object} in $\mathcal{A}$ --- the observable admits no non‑trivial decomposition to any other observable.
This categorical insight then forms the basis for a network of functors that translate between the higher layers of the architecture, providing a rigorous compositional semantics for the entire framework.
All definitions are accompanied by explicit proofs, and the theoretical claims are corroborated by a machine‑verifiable implementation in Python.
\end{abstract}
