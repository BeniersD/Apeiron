\section{Conclusion}

We have presented APEIRON-Core, a multi-backend hardware abstraction layer engineered to support non-anthropocentric AI. The framework provides a unified interface to CPU, CUDA, FPGA, and quantum backends, enabling higher cognitive layers to execute continuous field operations transparently across the full spectrum of available computational matter. Key contributions include a pluggable factory with automatic backend selection, a comprehensive error-handling decorator with Prometheus observability, and a novel energy-aware cost model that embeds ethical resource constraints into the execution layer.

Experimental results demonstrate that the HAL introduces less than 2.3\% overhead on realistic workloads, that GPU acceleration yields $>40\times$ speedups for large fields, and that FPGA offload reduces energy consumption by up to $8\times$ compared to CPU. The quantum backend achieves high-fidelity overlap measurements and efficient partial trace operations. The energy monitor successfully enforces carbon-intensity thresholds, proving the feasibility of thermodynamically self-aware computation.

APEIRON-Core forms the foundational execution substrate for the broader APEIRON framework, and its open-source release invites the community to extend it to new hardware targets and to integrate it into their own autonomous systems.
